2. ***
   KRTC Model: Integrated Progression Framework

The KRTC model is structured as a progressive and interconnected system that links different stages of education, training, and application:

Foundation Level
Academic preparation, early research exposure, language development, and community engagement support students at the secondary level.

Applied Science Training
Students transition into laboratory-based and problem-oriented learning across applied scientific fields, developing practical competence.

Research Engagement
Participants engage in research-oriented activities, including academic writing, methodology training, and project-based work.

Industry and Community Interface
Training is connected to technical applications, environmental initiatives, and community-based activities, linking science to real-world contexts.

Higher Education and Professional Pathways
The system supports progression toward advanced academic study and professional environments through skill development and academic preparation.

This integrated structure enables continuous movement between education, research, and application, supporting both individual development and broader capacity building.

3. ***
   Concept Statement

The Kekuleon Research and Training Center (KRTC) develops a decentralised and integrated institutional model designed to address the gap between theoretical education and practical scientific competence in developing-country contexts. The approach combines foundation-level preparation, applied science training, research engagement, and community-linked activities within a single operational framework.

By structuring learning as a progressive system, the model supports student transitions across different educational stages while strengthening readiness for both higher education and professional environments. At the same time, it introduces problem-oriented research and applied training aligned with environmental, social, and industrial contexts.

Rather than treating education, research, and application as separate domains, KRTC integrates them into a continuous learning environment. This approach aims to contribute to broader discussions on science education, capacity development, and the role of applied training systems in supporting sustainable development.

4. ***
   About KRTC

The Kekuleon Research and Training Center (KRTC) is an emerging initiative in Bangladesh focused on strengthening the connection between science education and practical competence. It responds to a common challenge in many developing contexts, where theoretical learning is not sufficiently supported by laboratory training, research exposure, or industry-oriented experience.

KRTC develops a decentralised and integrated institutional approach that brings together foundation education, applied science training, research engagement, and community involvement within a single framework. The aim is to support students in progressing from structured academic learning to applied and practice-based scientific work.

What We Do

KRTC operates through a progressive learning structure:

Foundation Program (KRTC Schooling)
Supports secondary-level students through academic reinforcement, early research exposure, language preparation, and structured engagement activities.

Applied Science Training
Provides laboratory-oriented and problem-focused learning across areas such as chemistry, materials science, pharmaceutical sciences, and healthcare.

Research and Academic Development
Introduces students and graduates to research methods, academic writing, and project-based learning.

Industry and Community Engagement
Connects training with real-world contexts through technical services, environmental initiatives, and community-based activities.

Our Approach

KRTC is based on the idea that education, research, and application are more effective when developed together. The model integrates:

academic preparation and progression

applied and laboratory-based learning

research exposure and skill development

industry-relevant training

community engagement linked to social and environmental needs

This approach supports not only knowledge acquisition but also the development of practical scientific competence and independent learning capacity.

Current Development

KRTC is currently operating through its foundation program, with students engaged in academic preparation, research awareness, language training, and community activities. The broader institutional framework is being developed in phases, with plans to expand participation and strengthen applied training and research components.

Research Orientation

KRTC also functions as a practice-based research initiative, exploring how decentralised training environments can contribute to improving science education and capacity development. It generates insight into:

the relationship between theory and practical skills

student transitions across education levels

the role of integrated training systems

connections between education, industry, and community needs

Vision

KRTC aims to develop an adaptable institutional model that strengthens applied science education and supports scientific capacity development in developing-country contexts. The approach is designed to remain flexible, allowing it to respond to different regional and institutional needs while maintaining a focus on practical learning, academic progression, and long-term impact.

5. ***
   The Problem We Address

In many developing-country contexts, science education faces several interconnected challenges:

Theory–practice gap
Education is largely theory-oriented, with limited access to laboratory training, applied research, and real-world scientific practice.

Limited research exposure
Many students engage in review-based academic work rather than experimental or problem-oriented research.

Mismatch with industry needs
Academic curricula are often not aligned with current industry and technical requirements.

Graduate employability challenges
Fresh graduates are frequently rejected by employers due to a lack of practical skills and hands-on experience.

Barriers to higher education mobility
Insufficient research training, academic writing skills, and laboratory experience limit students’ readiness for international study and research environments.

Weak integration between sectors
Limited collaboration between education, industry, and research institutions reduces opportunities for applied learning.

Unequal access to opportunities
Students from diverse socio-economic backgrounds often lack access to applied training, research environments, and academic support.

These challenges collectively reflect a broader structural issue: a disconnect between education, practice, and opportunity, which limits both individual progression and wider scientific capacity development.

Our Solution

KRTC addresses these challenges through a decentralised and integrated institutional model that connects education, research, and application within a single framework:

Progressive learning pathway
A structured system that supports students from foundation-level preparation to applied science training and research engagement.

Early research exposure
Introduction to scientific thinking, research methods, and academic skills from the secondary level.

Applied and laboratory-based training
Hands-on learning in fields such as chemistry, materials science, pharmaceutical sciences, and healthcare.

Problem-oriented research approach
Departments engage in research activities aligned with real-world challenges, including sustainability, environmental concerns, and industry-related needs.

Alignment with industry practices
Training is linked to technical skills, applied work, and industry-relevant competencies.

Support for higher education pathways
Preparation in academic writing, research skills, and language training to improve readiness for national and international study.

Integrated research and training environment
Students, graduates, and trainees engage in a system where learning, research, and application are developed together.

Focus on access and inclusion
The model expands opportunities for students from diverse socio-economic backgrounds through structured support and engagement.
